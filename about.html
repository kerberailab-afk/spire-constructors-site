Owner-Operated Isn't
a Buzzword Here

No corner office, no clipboard from a safe distance — just two owners knee-deep in the South Florida brush on another groundbreaking.
By Spire Constructors  ·  Field Report  ·  Photographs From The Site
Bill
Gary
On site: co-owners Bill Breunig and Gary Kerber at a recent South Florida groundbreaking. Boots muddy, bug spray wearing thin, still grinning.

On a humid morning at the edge of a South Florida build site — surveyors setting their stakes, grass standing taller than the trucks — the two men who run the company were exactly where they tend to be: out in it. That is the whole idea behind Spire Constructors, a general contractor where the owners are not signatures on a letterhead but the people on the ground.

It is a simple promise, and an increasingly rare one. The principals do not send a proxy to the hard meetings or the muddy mornings. They walk the site, read the dirt, and answer for the result — because their names are on it.

William “Bill” Breunig30+ years · multimillion-dollar construction

For more than three decades, Bill Breunig has managed every phase of multimillion-dollar construction — high-rises and luxury condominiums, infrastructure, corporate interiors, and healthcare facilities. A veteran of Plaza Construction Corp., one of New York's premier builders, he oversaw more than $500 million in projects.

Along the way he built a reputation for ironclad cost control, sharp contract negotiation, and careful subcontractor selection — plus the kind of client relations that turn first-time owners into lifelong ones. The discipline shows up early: most jobs, he will tell you, are won or lost long before the first shovel hits dirt.

Gary Kerber20+ years · development to commercial build-outs

Gary Kerber brings more than twenty years across every scale of construction — from ground-up residential development to large-scale renovations and commercial build-outs. A licensed real estate broker with deep knowledge of zoning and entitlement law, he reads a land-development code as easily as a set of structural drawings.

His specialty is scaling construction the modern way: through technology. He keeps schedules honest, coordinates vendors and subcontractors, and gives clients a level of day-to-day visibility most contractors never bother to offer.

“Build it like your name is on it — because it is.”

Between them sits roughly fifty years of building and a single shared rule: be on site, be straight with people, and deliver the job as if the family name rode on every detail. It does.

Serving all of Florida, Spire Constructors is owner-operated in the only way that truly counts — the owners show up, boots and all. ■
