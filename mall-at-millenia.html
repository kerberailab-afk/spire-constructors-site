The Project

We built everything the buildings stand on.

Spire took down an aging bank at Orlando's Mall at Millenia and delivered the complete site and infrastructure for two new retail pads — the future homes of Chase Bank and Diamonds Direct. The tenant buildings are theirs. Everything below grade and across the site is ours.

That meant the work most people never see: domestic and sanitary sewer, potable and reclaim water, an 8-inch underground fire-water system, and electrical feeds from the transformer to each building — then curb and gutter, paving, flatwork, site lighting, striping, landscaping, irrigation, and the trash enclosure. A complete site package, self-managed from demolition to final striping.

Start to Finish

How the site came together.

01 — Demo & Clearing

Down with the old bank

An aging bank stood on the pad. We demolished it and cleared the site to grade — a clean canvas for two new buildings.

02 — Underground Utilities

The work you never see

Domestic and sanitary sewer, potable and reclaim water, an 8-inch fire-water main, and electrical feeds from the transformer to each building — the full wet-and-dry utility package, all below grade.

03 — Curb, Gutter & Flatwork

Shaping the site

Curb and gutter, sidewalks, and concrete flatwork gave the site its structure — defining every drive, walkway, and building approach.

04 — Paving

Laying the lot

Asphalt paving tied the pads, drive aisles, and parking together into a finished surface.

05 — Site Finishes

The details that finish a site

Site lighting, striping, landscaping, irrigation, and a finished masonry trash enclosure brought the project home.

06 — Delivered

Two pads, ready for business

A complete, turnkey site — handed over for Chase Bank and Diamonds Direct to open their doors.
